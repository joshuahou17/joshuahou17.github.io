Day 339 of 365

2 Corinthians 5-9

Ambassadors of Reconciliation and the Grace of Giving

Read 2 Corinthians 5-9 in the NIV →

Context

Chapters 5-9 move from the foundation of Paul's ministry to a concrete appeal. In chapter 5 he looks past present groaning toward a heavenly dwelling, lives in light of the judgment seat of Christ, and articulates the heart of his message: God was in Christ reconciling the world to Himself, making the sinless one to be sin so that we might become the righteousness of God. Believers are therefore Christ's ambassadors, entrusted with the ministry of reconciliation.

Chapter 6 appeals for an open-hearted response and calls for separation from compromising alliances, while chapter 7 expresses Paul's joy that the painful letter produced godly grief leading to repentance. Chapters 8-9 then turn to the collection for the impoverished believers in Jerusalem. Paul points to the Macedonian churches, who gave generously out of deep poverty, and ultimately to Christ, who though rich became poor so that we might become rich, urging cheerful, unforced generosity that God will supply and multiply.

Key themes

New creation and reconciliation. If anyone is in Christ there is a new creation; the old has passed and the new has come. God reconciles us to Himself and commissions us as ambassadors carrying that same appeal to the world.

The great exchange. God made Christ, who knew no sin, to be sin for us, so that in Him we might become the righteousness of God. The whole ministry of reconciliation rests on this substitution.

The grace of giving. Generosity is itself a grace, seen in Macedonians whose joy overflowed beyond their poverty and in Christ's self-impoverishment. God loves a cheerful giver and supplies seed to the one who sows.

Takeaways

  • To be in Christ is to be a new creation and an ambassador, entrusted with announcing that God reconciles sinners to Himself through the cross.
  • The gospel's logic is exchange: the sinless Christ bore our sin so that we receive His righteousness, which is the message reconciliation rests upon.
  • Generosity is a grace empowered by God, measured not by amount but by cheerful, sacrificial willingness, modeled by the Macedonians and by Christ Himself.

Connections

The ministry of reconciliation extends the prophetic vision of God gathering and restoring His scattered people, and the new creation language anticipates the renewed heavens and earth promised in Isaiah and Revelation. Christ becoming poor for our sake echoes the self-giving servant of Isaiah 53. The collection for the poor fulfills the consistent biblical concern for the needy, and Paul's principle that God loves a cheerful giver draws on the wisdom of Proverbs about generosity and provision.

Isaiah 65:17 → Isaiah 53:4-6 → Proverbs 11:24-25 → Deuteronomy 15:7-11 → Galatians 6:9-10 →

Reflection

If you really are an ambassador, what message is your life delivering on Christ's behalf to the people who only meet Him through you? And consider your giving: not the figure, but the freedom behind it. The Macedonians gave joyfully out of poverty because they had first grasped how much they had received. Does gratitude or anxiety hold the purse?
